Jeremy Segal

I am a musician, composer, and audio engineer based in Perth, Western Australia.

Electronic Music

I make ambient and experimental electronic music, with a particular interest in process-based composition and semi-generative systems. In 2020 I presented Four Footprints, a collection of ambient and environmental pieces, in six-channel surround sound at Callaway Auditorium. In 2021 I released Plateaux, an EP exploring audible process and transformation via aesthetic elements of ambient, techno, and noise. I am currently working on a collection of multi-channel works exploring algorithmic treatments of the voice, which will be presented at Callaway Auditorium on June 16th at 7pm, as well as a commissioned piece for Audible Edge 2022, which will be performed at Old Customs House, Fremantle in September.

I have recently performed live electronic sets at WA Museum Boola Bardip, Outcome Unknown, Weekend At Bernie's Festival, The Bird and Tomich House, and with Seacrest Gardens at Fremantle Biennale, Goolugatup Heathcote, and Audible Edge.

Bands

Segue was an indie-rock band turned synth-pop duo active from 2015 to 2022. We released two EPs and two albums. My personal favourite is our first full length Primary Ties. We supported touring artists including Cate le Bon, Methyl Ethel, and Deep Sea Arcade.

Didion's Bible is a rock band active since 2017. You can check out our music here.

Studio Work

I have produced, recorded, and mixed music in a range of styles and contexts, from synth-pop to indie-rock, folk, and live performance.
